# Family Business 🚬

A turn-based mob-empire game in a single HTML file. Found a crime family, take the city block by block, and climb from **Associate** to **Don of the City** — with d20 rolls behind every major move and rival families that earn, spend, fight and scheme on their own clocks.

## Play

Open `index.html` in any browser — no install, no build step. Progress autosaves in your browser.

## Found your family

A new game starts with **character creation**: name your boss and family, pick a background (Enforcer / Grifter / Diplomat / Bookkeeper), and spread 5 points across four skills:

- 🔨 **Force** — muscle, takeovers, holding ground
- 🦊 **Cunning** — jobs, sabotage, staying ahead of the law
- 🗣️ **Influence** — diplomacy, buyouts, friends in high places
- 📒 **Business** — ledgers, upgrades, making money make money

Every 2 skill levels add **+1 to the d20** behind every major action. Rank-ups grant skill points and, at skill 3 / 6 / 9, a **perk choice** (two options) from the 👤 Boss screen — 24 perks in all.

## The city

Seven **districts**, each split into three **neighborhoods**, each holding three **locations** (63 in total). You start with a foothold in **Little Harbor**. The four middle districts are held by local **thugs**; the **Morettis** rule the Ironworks and the **Volkovs** hold Zarechye.

The conquest ladder is strictly bottom-up:

- Take **locations** one at a time — buy the open ones, **🔫 Muscle** thug/rival fronts (Force check), or **🤝 Buy** them out peacefully (Influence check, no heat).
- Hold a **majority of a neighborhood's locations** to unlock a **⚔️ Take Block** assault that seizes the rest in one night.
- Hold a **majority of a district's neighborhoods** to unlock a **🏴 Take District** assault.
- Every assault is a Force roll. **Fail and you lose crew** — goons first, then capos — scaling with the level of the attempt, and the target fortifies against you.

## Living rivals

The Morettis and Volkovs are real competitors: each has its own **cash, goons, capos, and weekly actions**. They earn income from their turf, expand into what they can afford, fight for territory, and **never spend money they don't have**. Aggression and tactics differ by family — Moretti is warlike, Volkov schemes.

You see only rough estimates of their strength until you **🔎 Scout** them. Then choose your road:

- **Offensive** — invade, take blocks and districts by force.
- **Diplomatic** — **🕊️ truces**, **🤝 alliances** (joint pressure on the other family), and **👑 vassalage** (force a beaten family to pay you weekly tribute instead of dying).
- **Economic** — stack location **upgrades** (💰 Till income, 🛡️ Guards defense, 🎭 Front heat-reduction), rackets, and district-control bonuses into a money machine.
- **Sabotage** — **🧨** torch rival fronts and drain their war chest from the shadows, drawing far less heat than a gunfight.

Strip a family of **all its districts**, then hit their last safehouse in a **🎯 Final Hit** to end them for good. Fail it and you hand them a martyr and a fresh war chest.

## The catch

Squeezing, rackets and muscle all draw **heat**. At 100 the city cracks down — cash seized, a location shuttered, a goon lost. Three crackdowns is a **RICO case**: game over. Go more than $5,000 into debt and the loan sharks collect instead.

Win by reaching **Don of the City** at 1,200 respect — by conquest, diplomacy, gold, or any mix that gets you there.
